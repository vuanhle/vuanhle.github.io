# Research

<p class="lead">My research sits at the intersection of reliable machine learning, AI for science, and technical systems where errors have real downstream consequences.</p>

## Research Agenda

- **Reliable Machine Learning:** studying how models behave under uncertainty, distribution shift, contamination, and scientific constraints.
- **LLM Reliability:** developing lightweight signals for identifying contaminated or memorized data in large model workflows.
- **AI for Science:** building learning systems for scientific discovery where structure, feasibility, and interpretability matter.
- **Remote Sensing and Risk:** using satellite intelligence and geospatial analysis to understand urban and infrastructure risk.
- **Constrained Generative Modeling:** designing models whose outputs are not just plausible, but valid under domain constraints.

## Research Taste

I am drawn to problems where pure benchmark performance is not enough. The systems I care about must be auditable, robust, and useful to researchers, operators, investors, and decision-makers. That means asking both theoretical questions and deployment questions: what signal is the model using, when does it fail, and what would make it trustworthy enough for scientific or economic decisions?

## Current Direction

At UVA, I plan to deepen this agenda through work on reliable AI systems, scientific machine learning, and the infrastructure needed to evaluate and deploy frontier models responsibly.
